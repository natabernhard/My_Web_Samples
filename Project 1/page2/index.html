From the beginning until the present - the history of cats.

About 40,000,000 (forty million) years ago, there was a time when there were no cats in the world. Absolutely none. There were many other animals. One group of animals was called "Miacids," and it gradually evolved into the first cat. But this took millions of years. It was like the slow movement of transformers. This is how nature works, and although even today many people disagree with this, the majority still believe it. The way animals gradually change over millions of years is an important subject. It's called "evolution." Animals "evolve." Here's what happens. Sometimes, when animals are born, they are different from their parents. This can be a mistake of nature. But if this newborn can survive more easily because it's different, it will also be better at passing on this difference to its offspring, and so there will be more animals of this type. And then, there will only be animals of this type. This process takes a very long time. It turned out that the first animal in the world that truly looked like a cat and walked like a cat lived around 10,000,000 (ten million) years ago. It was called the pseudoelurus.
This cat lived in Europe, Asia, and North America. It had a strange name, but people who knew cats and animals very well liked to give names to animals using an old language called Latin. This made them sound more important. These animals no longer exist in the world because they evolved into other animals. Then, very gradually, a cat named Martelli Cat appeared in the world. He lived in Europe about two and a half million years ago. Around the same time, saber-toothed tigers lived in the world. Saber-toothed tigers are not real tigers. This big cat is no longer around. It completely disappeared about 10,000 years ago. The saber-toothed tiger could not survive. Cat experts believe that the first lions, leopards, cheetahs, and jaguars in the world lived around the same time as Martelli Cat (2.5 million years ago). The tiger appeared about one and a half million years ago. The dates are not certain. Martelli Cat evolved into many modern small wild cats. One of these small wild cats, called the African wildcat, had the courage to live near humans because people were farmers, and the cat enjoyed eating the mice that fed on the grain grown by the farmers. Gradually, the wildcat and humans became friends because both made each other's lives a little better. The cat killed rats, which helped the farmer, and the cat's reward was food. It was a good deal. This first happened about 9,500 years ago in a place on the eastern side of the Mediterranean Sea. The result of this friendship was the world's first domesticated cats. We know this happened because a burial site was found with a human and an African wildcat at his feet. The grave was about 9,500 years old.
Ancient Egyptians were people who truly domesticated many cats. The domestic cat was very useful for eliminating rats and snakes. About 3000 years ago, the domestic cat became a part of the Egyptians' lives. They became so integral to their lives that they worshiped the cat, elevating it to the status of a god. But in worshiping the cat, they also committed bad deeds, sacrificing cats to please their feline deity. Today, Egyptians still keep cats, and they are known as Egyptian Maus. The domestic cat was a helpful animal companion 3000 years ago. This led to cats traveling with people who embarked on journeys to distant places to sell goods. The domestic cat spread to other parts of the world. The first domestic cats appeared in India about 1500 years ago and in Russia about 1300 years ago. Romans brought domestic cats to Britain when they invaded the island about 1900 years ago. North America was one of the last places where domestic cats could be seen. When people from Europe arrived on ships to settle in America 300-400 years ago, they brought ship cats with them. Cats were useful mousers both on ships and on land. Some of the early cats that came to people from Europe gradually became the first Maine Coon and American Shorthair cats. Both of these cats are popular breeds today. Between their arrival on the East Coast of America and today, they served as barn cats, catching rats and mice. Both wild and domestic cats, being talented at survival and mysterious in their nocturnal behaviors, have simultaneously fascinated and instilled fear. The founder of the Muslim faith, Prophet Muhammad, who lived about 1400 years ago, lived with a cat named Muadz in Damascus. Muhammad respected and loved cats, and for this reason, cats are allowed in mosques today. Around the same time, in Peru and Chile, people worshipped the god of the puma. The puma is a large wild cat that still inhabits some parts of Chile in South America. In Buddhist temples, cats are kept as mouse hunters. The Christian religion had both positive and negative impacts on cats. Thousands of years ago, during the time of Jesus, wild cats lived in Israel, but there is no mention of cats in the Bible. In 1232 (780 years ago as of 2012), the Pope of the Roman Catholic Church declared that people who did not believe but instead worshipped the devil, and he said the devil was a black cat. Now it sounds absurd, but back then, it was taken very seriously. Over the next 460 years, the poor cat was persecuted by the Christian Church. Even as recently as 1692, in Salem, Americans stood trial as witches. These dark times for cats are now in the past, and cats are generally loved today. However, there are still many people today who fear and dislike cats. Around the mid-1800s (about 160 years ago), people who truly loved cats decided to create a club to show off their cats to each other and others. They believed they could enhance the appearance of their cats by ensuring that specific cats mated with others they liked and selected. This led to the creation of purebred cat breeds. These individuals are part of what is known as the "Cat Fancy."
